    Beginner Tips

    Quick advice to help you improve and stay motivated.

    New to badminton?

    Start with learning basic grip and clear shots before advanced techniques.

    Struggling with serve?

    Practice low serves close to the net and focus on consistency.

    Missing shots often?

    Improve footwork and positioning before increasing power.

    Getting tired quickly?

    Take short breaks and focus on controlled rallies.

    Wants to improve faster?

    Play 1 to 2 times per week and practice basic drills.

    Nervous during games?

    Focus on keeping the shuttle in play rather than winning points.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Get answers to common questions about badminton.

    Badminton is a racket sport played by hitting a shuttlecock over a net. Players score points by making the shuttlecock land inside their opponent's court. It can be played indoors or outdoors, either one-on-one (singles) or two-on-two (doubles).

    Yes! Badminton is beginner-friendly, easy to learn, and suitable for all ages. You can start playing with basic equipment and simple rules, then improve your skills over time.

    You only need: badminton racket, shuttlecock, comfortable sports clothing, indoor court shoes (recommended).

    Serve diagonally, shuttlecock must be hit below waist height, racket shaft must point downward, both feet must stay inside the service court.

    Clear: High shot to the back of the court. Drop: Soft shot close to the net. Smash: Powerful downward shot. Drive: Fast, flat shot.
