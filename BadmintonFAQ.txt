
Q: What is badminton?
A: Badminton is a racket sport played by hitting a shuttlecock over a net. Players score points by making the shuttlecock land inside their opponent’s court. It can be played indoors or outdoors, either one-on-one (singles) or two-on-two (doubles).

Q: Is badminton suitable for beginners?
A: Yes! Badminton is beginner-friendly, easy to learn, and suitable for all ages. You can start playing with basic equipment and simple rules, then improve your skills over time.

Q: What are the benefits of playing badminton?
A:

Improves fitness and stamina

Enhances hand-eye coordination

Builds leg strength and agility

Burns calories

Reduces stress

Improves reaction speed

Q: What equipment do I need to play badminton?
A: 
You only need:

badminton racket

 shuttlecock

Comfortable sports clothing

Indoor court shoes (recommended)

Q: What is a shuttlecock?
A: A shuttlecock (also called a birdie) is the object hit during the game. It has a rounded base and feathers or plastic that slow it down in the air.

Q: What is the difference between feather and plastic shuttlecocks?
A:

Plastic shuttlecock: Durable, cheaper, best for beginners

Feather shuttlecock: Used in competitions, more control, less durable

Q: What kind of racket should a beginner use?
A: Beginners should use:

Lightweight racket

Flexible shaft

Comfortable grip size
These help reduce arm strain and make hitting easier.

Q: What does a badminton court look like?
A: A badminton court is rectangular and divided by a net. There are different lines for singles and doubles games.

Q: How high is the badminton net?
A:

At the center: 1.524 m

At the sides: 1.55 m

Q: What are singles and doubles courts?
A:

Singles: Narrower court

Doubles: Wider court
Different side lines are used depending on the game type.

Q: How do you start a badminton game?
A: The game starts with a serve, where one player hits the shuttlecock diagonally across the net into the opponent’s service box.

Q: How is scoring done in badminton?
A:

Rally scoring system

Every rally earns 1 point

First to 21 points wins a game

Must win by 2 points

Q: How many games are played in a match?
A: A match is played as best of 3 games.

Q: What is a rally?
A: A rally is the sequence of shots exchanged between players until one side makes a mistake or scores.

Q: How do I serve in badminton?
A:

Serve diagonally

Shuttlecock must be hit below waist height

Racket shaft must point downward

Both feet must stay inside the service court

Q: When do I change service courts?
A:

Even score → serve from right court

Odd score → serve from left court

Q: What are the basic badminton shots?
A:

Clear: High shot to the back of the court

Drop: Soft shot close to the net

Smash: Powerful downward shot

Drive: Fast, flat shot

Q: Which shot should beginners focus on first?
A: Beginners should focus on:

Clear

Drop

Basic serve
These build control and consistency.

Q: What mistakes do beginners often make?
A:

Hitting too hard

Standing still instead of moving

Holding the racket too tightly

Poor foot positioning

Incorrect serve height

Q: How can I avoid injuries while playing badminton?
A:

Warm up before playing

Stretch after playing

Wear proper court shoes

Stay hydrated

Stop if you feel pain


Q: Is badminton hard on the knees?
A: Badminton involves jumping and lunging, so proper shoes and technique help protect your knees.

Q: How often should beginners play badminton?
A:

1–2 times per week for beginners

Increase gradually as fitness improves

Q: How can I improve faster as a beginner?
A:

Practice footwork

Learn correct grip

Play with more experienced players

Watch tutorial videos

Focus on consistency, not power

Q: What is good badminton etiquette?
A:

Shake hands after games

Do not distract opponents

Respect line calls

Share the court fairly

Q: Can I play badminton casually without knowing all rules?
A: Yes! Casual play is about fun. Rules become more important in competitive matches.

Q: Is badminton an Olympic sport?
A: Yes, badminton is an Olympic sport played internationally.


Q: Can children and seniors play badminton?
A: Yes. Badminton can be adapted for all ages and fitness levels.
